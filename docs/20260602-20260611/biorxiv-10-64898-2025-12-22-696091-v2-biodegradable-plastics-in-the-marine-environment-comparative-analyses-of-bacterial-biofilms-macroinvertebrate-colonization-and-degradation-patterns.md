## 摘要
海洋中的漂浮碎片会招募微生物和海洋无脊椎动物附着在其表面，形成漂流群落。随着生物可降解塑料作为石油基塑料的替代品日益普及，其特性可能通过影响细菌生物膜的组成和附着无脊椎动物，从而影响漂流群落的动态。我们比较了生物可降解表面、不可降解的石油基塑料和天然基质上的附着生物污损群落，包括细菌和海洋无脊椎动物。对六种表面类型：聚丙烯塑料（PP）、聚苯乙烯（PS）、聚乳酸塑料（PLA）、聚羟基烷酸酯塑料（PHA）、枫木薄板和红树（Rhizophora mangle）的繁殖体进行了研究，以确定塑料类型是否影响表面生物膜组成及其降解模式。在浸泡十二周时进行生物膜分析，二十二周时进行降解分析。利用数字图像分析、16S rRNA测序和宏基因组分析，我们发现微生物生物膜、海洋无脊椎动物栖息者和降解模式在所测试的不同基质类型间存在差异。PLA上的微生物群落与两种不可降解塑料上的更为相似，而PHA上的群落则更类似于天然基质。生物可降解的PHA在浸泡22周内显示出降解迹象，表明生物可降解塑料在自然环境中的表现各异。这项研究的结果强调了设计生物可降解塑料时需要仔细考虑其可能持续存在的环境条件的重要性。

## Abstract
Floating debris in the ocean recruits microbes and marine invertebrates to its surface, resulting in rafting communities. As biodegradable plastics increase in prevalence as alternatives to petroleum-based plastics, their properties may impact the dynamics of rafting communities by influencing the composition of bacterial biofilms and attached invertebrates. We compared attached biofouling communities of bacteria and marine invertebrates on biodegradable surfaces and nonbiodegradable petroleum-based plastics and naturally occurring substrata. Six surface types: polypropylene plastic (PP), polystyrene (PS), polylactic acid plastic (PLA), polyhydroxyalkanoates plastic (PHA), maple wood veneer, and propagules of the mangrove Rhizophora mangle, were examined to determine if plastic type affected biofilm composition on the surfaces and their degradation patterns. Biofilm analyses were conducted at twelve weeks, and degradation analyses were conducted at twenty-two weeks of immersion. Using digital image analysis, 16S rRNA sequencing, and metagenomic analyses, we found that microbial biofilms, marine invertebrate inhabitants, and degradation patterns differed across the various substrate types tested. Microbial communities on PLA were more similar to those on the two non-biodegradable plastics, while communities on PLA were more like those on the natural substrata. The biodegradable PLA showed signs of degradation within 22 weeks of immersion, suggesting that biodegradable plastics behave variably in the natural environment. Results of this study bring forth the importance of designing biodegradable plastics with careful consideration of the environmental conditions in which they are likely to persist.
