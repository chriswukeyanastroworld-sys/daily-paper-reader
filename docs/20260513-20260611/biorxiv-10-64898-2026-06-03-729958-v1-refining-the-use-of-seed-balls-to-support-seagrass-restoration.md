## 摘要
海草生态系统正在经历全球范围内的快速衰退，当前基于种子的恢复努力经常受到严重的早期生命瓶颈（如十足类捕食和生物扰动）的阻碍。为了绕过这些生态反馈，将大叶藻（Zostera marina）种子包裹在保护性黏土基质（种子球）中，为可规模化部署提供了一种有前景的低成本中间技术。为改进此方法，开展了一项中宇宙实验，评估不同黏土配方（耐火黏土、膨润土和凯特林壤土）及干燥处理下大叶藻种子的物理完整性、萌发成功率和早期幼苗发育。种子球被置于沉积物表面或埋入其中，并使用障碍型广义加性模型方法评估其表现。结果表明，结构稳健性与生物性能之间存在明显的权衡。虽然裸露种子（萌发率70.3%）和埋入的耐火黏土球（萌发率67.2%）展现出最高的总体萌发率和萌发后幼苗长度，但含有较高比例膨润土和壤土的表面部署种子球（如2:4:1比例）表现出优越的结构完整性，能抵抗部署过程中的降解。然而，与裸露种子相比，这些较致密的基质显著降低了萌发概率并限制了幼苗发育。缺乏壤土的配方（1:3）在所有生物和结构指标上表现最差。尽管相对于埋入处理萌发率降低，表面部署的种子球仍实现了高活力，优于典型的田间萌发率。虽然还需进一步的现场试验以在动态水动力和生物扰动压力下测试配方，但种子球提供了一种科学稳健、可扩展的机制，以绕过早期捕食并支持广泛的海景恢复。

## Abstract
Seagrass ecosystems are experiencing rapid global decline, and current seed-based restoration efforts are frequently hindered by severe early-life bottlenecks such as decapod predation and bioturbation. To bypass these ecological feedbacks, encasing Zostera marina seeds in protective clay matrices (seed balls) presents a promising, low-cost intermediate technology for scalable deployment. To refine this approach, a mesocosm experiment was conducted to evaluate the physical integrity, germination success, and early shoot development of Z. marina seeds across various clay formulations (fireclay, bentonite, and Kettering loam) and drying treatments. Seed balls were deployed either on the sediment surface or buried, and their performance was evaluated using a hurdle-style generalised additive modelling approach. Results indicated a clear trade-off between structural robustness and biological performance. While naked seeds (70.3% emergence) and buried fireclay balls (67.2% emergence) exhibited the highest overall emergence and post-emergence shoot length, surface-deployed seed balls formulated with higher proportions of bentonite and loam (e.g., 2:4:1 ratio) demonstrated the superior structural integrity necessary for resisting degradation during deployment. However, these denser matrices significantly reduced emergence probabilities and restricted shoot development compared to naked seeds. Formulations lacking loam (1:3) performed the poorest across all biological and structural metrics. Despite reduced emergence relative to buried treatments, surface-deployed seed balls still achieved high viability, outperforming typical field germination rates. Although further field-based refinement is required to test formulations under dynamic hydrodynamic and bioturbation pressures, seed balls offer a scientifically robust, scalable mechanism to bypass early-stage predation and support widespread seascape recovery.
